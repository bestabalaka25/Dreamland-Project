      Scotland's World Cup Journey

      SCOTLAND'S WORLD CUP HISTORY

      Scotland's World Cup story began in 1954 with their very first appearance in Switzerland. From there, the nation qualified for eight tournaments across four decades — 1954, 1958, 1974, 1978, 1982, 1986, 1990 and 1998 — each campaign carrying the hopes of an entire nation, and each ending at the group stage in circumstances that still sting today.

      The 1974 draw with Brazil, Archie Gemmill's breathtaking solo goal against the Netherlands in 1978, and the emotional farewell of France 1998 are moments etched into Scottish football history. Since 1998, qualification has proved elusive — but the dream remains alive, and Scotland continue to fight for a return to the World Cup stage in 2026 and beyond.

      Tartan Army

      NO SCOTLAND, NO PARTY

      From Wembley in 1977 to France 98, the Tartan Army turned Scotland's football journey into a travelling celebration of song, colour, and hope. Win or lose, the supporters made every tournament feel bigger than the scoreboard.

      "No Scotland, no party" became a rallying cry for generations of fans who followed the team across Europe and beyond, bringing blue shirts, bagpipes, flags, and unstoppable energy.

      What is the Dreamland Project?

      Dreamland is an interactive web documentary celebrating Scotland's FIFA World Cup journey from 1974 to 1998. It combines editorial storytelling, player profiles, a match timeline, and fan stories all in one site.

      How do I navigate between sections?

      Use the navigation bar at the top of the page. Click HOME, TIMELINE, PLAYERS, NO PARTY, STORIES or FAQS to jump to that section. The nav stays visible as you scroll.

      How do I view a player's full profile?

      Go to the Players section and click any player card. A profile modal will open showing their biography, career era, position and achievements. Click the X or outside the modal to close it.

      Can I watch player highlights?

      Yes. On each player card, click the arrow on the right to swipe to a video highlights panel. Click the left arrow to go back to the photo. On mobile, swipe left or right directly on the card.

      How do I browse all the players?

      The Players section is split across two pages. Use the Prev and Next buttons below the cards, or click the dots to jump directly to a page.

      How do I scroll through the fan stories?

      Use the arrow buttons below the Stories section to scroll left and right through the fan story cards and read experiences from supporters across the years.

      Can I submit my own story?

      Yes. Click the Share Your Story button below the stories section, fill in your details and memory, and your story will appear alongside the others immediately after submitting.

      Does the site work on mobile?

      Yes. The site is designed to work across modern browsers on both desktop and mobile. Player card swiping and the FAQ carousel are both touch-enabled.

      What browsers does the site support?

      Dreamland works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Edge and Safari. Keep your browser up to date for the best experience.

      Who built this site?

      Dreamland was built by a student project team as part of a university web development module, covering full-stack development, frontend design, content research, and visual design.
